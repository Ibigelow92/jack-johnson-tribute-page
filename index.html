Jack Johnson

Legendary American Boxer

  • Born March 31, 1878 in Galveston, Texas.
  • Both of his parents were slaves.
  • Dropped out of school after five years, but learned to read and write.
  • Became a janitor at a boxing gym at age 16 and trained in his free time.
  • Won his first official match in 1898 against Charlie Brooks.
  • Rose through the ranks, beating former champion Bob Fitzsimmons and the brother of the champion at the time, Jim Jefferies.
  • Jim Jefferies retired, making way for a new heavyweight champion, Tommy Burns.
  • Despite the racist custom of not giving Black fighters a shot at the title, the new champion Tommy Burns was forced to fight Jack Johnson due to financial trouble.
  • Jack Johnson won the heavyweight championship against Tommy Burns in 1908, becoming the first Black world champion boxer.
